# Flood Insurance — Standard Flood Insurance Policy (Dwelling Form) — stand-in wording

## Definition of Flood
Flood means a general and temporary condition of partial or complete inundation of two or more acres of normally dry land area or of two or more properties, at least one of which is your property, from overflow of inland or tidal waters, from unusual and rapid accumulation or runoff of surface waters from any source, or from mudflow. Flood also means collapse or subsidence of land along the shore of a lake or similar body of water as a result of erosion or undermining caused by waves or currents of water exceeding anticipated cyclical levels.

## Property Covered — Coverage A Building Property
We insure against direct physical loss by or from flood to your insured building, meaning the dwelling at the described location, including additions and extensions attached to and in contact with the dwelling, and central air conditioning, furnaces, water heaters, and built-in appliances. We do not cover personal property unless Coverage B is shown on the Declarations.

## Coverage Limitations in Basements and Below the Lowest Elevated Floor
We do not pay for personal property in a basement or in an area below the lowest elevated floor of an elevated building, except for specified equipment connected to a power source and installed in its functioning location. In a basement, building coverage is limited to specified items such as the furnace, water heater, foundation walls, and unfinished drywall.

## Separation of Flood and Wind
This policy covers loss by flood only. Loss caused by wind, windstorm, or wind-driven rain that is not flood is not covered under this policy and is addressed, if at all, under the homeowners or businessowners policy. When wind and flood both contribute to a loss, the cause of each portion of the damage must be determined separately.

## What You Must Do After a Loss
You must give prompt written notice of loss, as soon as reasonably possible separate the damaged and undamaged property and put it in the best possible order for examination, and within 60 days after the loss send us a proof of loss, which is your statement of the amount you are claiming under the policy signed and sworn to by you.
